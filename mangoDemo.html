Alphonso: Known as the "king of mangoes," this variety is sweet and aromatic, and is grown mainly in India.


Grows

If you live in a region with a suitable climate, you may be able to grow Alphonso mangoes in your backyard. Here are the general steps to grow Alphonso mangoes:

Choose a suitable location: Alphonso mangoes require a warm and humid climate with plenty of sunlight. They prefer well-draining soil with a pH level between 5.5 and 7.5. Choose a location that receives plenty of sunlight and is protected from strong winds.

Plant the tree: Plant the Alphonso mango tree during the spring season. Dig a hole that is slightly larger than the root ball of the sapling and plant it in the hole. Backfill with soil and water the tree thoroughly.

Water and fertilize: Alphonso mango trees require regular watering, especially during the first two years after planting. Fertilize the tree every 2-3 months with a balanced fertilizer that contains nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.

Prune the tree: Prune the tree during the winter season to remove any dead or diseased branches. This will help to improve the overall health and shape of the

Harvest the mangoes: Alphonso mangoes typically ripen between April and June. Wait until the mangoes are fully ripe before harvesting them. This will ensure that they are sweet and flavorful.

Keep in mind that growing Alphonso mangoes requires a lot of care and attention, and it may take several years before the tree begins to bear fruit. However, if you are patient and diligent in caring for the tree, you may be able to enjoy delicious Alphonso mangoes from your own backyard.
