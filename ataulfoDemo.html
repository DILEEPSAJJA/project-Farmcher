About ataulfo mango

Mango trees grow to 30–40 metres (98–131 feet) tall, with a crown radius of 10–15 m (33–49 ft).

Growing Ataulfo mangoes can be a rewarding experience, but it requires some patience and care. If you are unsure about the specific requirements for growing Ataulfo mangoes in your area, consult with a local expert or agricultural extension service for more information.


Grows

Ataulfo mangoes can be grown in tropical or subtropical regions, and require a warm and humid climate to thrive. Here are some general guidelines for growing Ataulfo mangoes:

Climate: Ataulfo mangoes grow best in areas with a warm and humid climate, with temperatures ranging from 20-35°C (68-95°F) and an average annual rainfall of 1000-1500 mm. They can tolerate some drought conditions but do not tolerate frost.

Soil: Ataulfo mangoes grow well in well-drained soils that are rich in organic matter, with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5.

Propagation: Ataulfo mangoes can be propagated from seed, but it is recommended to use grafted plants as they are more likely to produce high-quality fruit. Grafted plants are usually available from nurseries.

Planting: Ataulfo mangoes should be planted in a sunny location, away from other trees or structures that may shade them. Dig a hole that is twice as wide and deep as the root ball, and backfill with a mixture of soil and organic matter.

Care: Ataulfo mangoes require regular watering, especially during dry periods. Fertilize with a balanced fertilizer once or twice a year, and prune regularly to maintain the shape of the tree and promote fruiting.

Harvest: Ataulfo mangoes are typically ready for harvest between March and July, depending on the location and climate. The fruit should be picked when fully ripe but still firm, and stored at room temperature until they are fully ripe and ready to eat.
